Things to do


Fiordland National Park offers a range of exciting activities that allow visitors to fully experience its breathtaking natural beauty Some of the top things to do in Fiordland include: Hiking and Tramping Fishing Wildlife Watching Caving and Exploring Mountain Biking etc. For hiking enthusiasts, the park is home to some of the world’s most famous tracks, including the Milford Track, Routeburn Track, and Kepler Track, each offering breathtaking views of the landscape.


Things we can do in fiordland.


Hiking

Hiking is refreshing and invigorating indoor or outdoor activity that allows individuals to connect with nature while improving physical fitness.


Hiking

Hiking is a popular outdoor activity that involves walking through natural environments, typically on trails or paths, to enjoy the beauty of nature, get exercise, and experience tranquility away from the hustle and bustle of daily life. It can vary in difficulty, from short, easy walks on well-maintained paths to challenging backcountry treks that require technical skills and preparation.

1.Types Of Hiking


>Day Hiking:
Short hikes that can be completed in a single day, often on marked trails.
Backpacking: Longer hikes that involve carrying gear and camping overnight, sometimes for multiple days.
Mountain Hiking:
Hiking in mountainous regions, which can include steep ascents, alpine environments, and sometimes challenging terrain.
Trail Running:
A more intense form of hiking that involves running on trails.
Urban Hiking:
Walking through natural or green spaces within a city or urban area.

2. Benefits of Hiking:

Physical Health:
Hiking is great for cardiovascular health, strength, and endurance. It works your legs, core, and improves flexibility.
Mental Health:
Spending time outdoors and disconnecting from daily stressors can improve mood, reduce anxiety, and increase overall well-being.
Connection with Nature:
Hiking allows you to immerse yourself in the natural world, which can deepen your appreciation for the environment and increase environmental awareness.

On the Water

Being on the water offers a unique sense of freedom and tranquility, whether you're boating, or simply enjoying the view from the shore.


Adventures

Both of these fiords can be explored by boat, allowing you to appreciate the steep mountain walls, dense forests, and wildlife in a way that's impossible on land.

1.Kayaking and Canoeing

For those who want a more personal, up-close experience with the fiords and waters of Fiordland, kayaking is a great option. You can kayak in Milford Sound, Doubtful Sound, and Lake Te Anau, one of the largest lakes in New Zealand. Kayaking gives you the freedom to explore quieter areas and spot wildlife while enjoying the peaceful environment.

2.Jet Boating

Fiordland is home to several rivers, including the Wairaurahiri River, which offers one of the most exhilarating jet boating experiences in New Zealand. The river flows through remote wilderness and is accessible only by boat, making it a thrilling way to see parts of the park that most visitors don’t reach.

3. Scenic Flights

If you want to combine the water and mountains from above, consider taking a scenic flight over Fiordland. Some flights even include a landing on a remote fiord, where you can embark on a boat trip to explore the surrounding waters.

Best Time to Visit:

The best time for a waterbound adventure in Fiordland is typically during the summer months (December to February) when the weather is warmer and the days are longer. However, Fiordland can be visited year-round, though the area is known for its rainfall, so it’s always a good idea to be prepared for changing weather.

Photography

Nature photography captures the beauty and wonder of the natural world. It involves framing the intricate details of landscapes.


Photography

Photography is both an art and a science, capturing light to create images that preserve moments, convey emotions, or communicate ideas. Here's a broad overview:

1.Basics of Photography

Aperture:
The size of the opening in the lens that lets light into the camera. A wider aperture (smaller f-number) lets in more light and creates a blurry background (shallow depth of field). A smaller aperture (larger f-number) allows less light and keeps more of the image in focus (greater depth of field).
Shutter Speed:
The length of time the camera's shutter is open to let light in. Fast shutter speeds (e.g., 1/1000 second) freeze motion, while slow shutter speeds (e.g., 1/30 second) can create a sense of movement.
ISO:
The camera sensor’s sensitivity to light. A lower ISO (e.g., 100 or 200) is best for bright conditions and results in clearer, sharper images, while higher ISO settings (e.g., 1600 or 3200) are useful in low light but can introduce noise or graininess.

2.Types of Photography

Portrait Photography:
Focuses on capturing the personality and mood of an individual or group. Good lighting and composition are key.
Landscape Photography: Capturing the beauty of the natural world, such as mountains, beaches, forests, or deserts. This often involves wide-angle lenses and careful consideration of natural light.
Street Photography:
Capturing candid moments in public places. It’s about documenting the human condition and life in an urban environment.
Sports Photography:
Involves fast shutter speeds and long lenses to capture action shots of athletes in motion.
Wildlife Photography:
Similar to nature photography but specifically focused on animals, often Product Photography: Specialized in capturing products for advertising or e-commerce. It focuses on lighting and presentation to make products look attractive.

3.Post-Processing (Editing)

Software:
Programs like Adobe Lightroom and Photoshop are commonly used to enhance photos. Editing can include adjusting exposure, contrast, white balance, cropping, and removing unwanted elements.
Filters and Presets:
Predefined adjustments to make images look a certain way. These are often used for consistency, especially on social media platforms like Instagram.

Adventure Sport

National park adventure sports offer an exciting and immersive way to experience the natural beauty and rugged landscapes.


Sports

1.Bungee Jumping (nearby Queenstown)

For a more laid-back yet equally breathtaking experience, take a scenic flight over Fiordland, providing an aerial view of the stunning fjords, lakes, and mountains. Some flights also offer the opportunity to land on glaciers for a unique hiking experience.

2.Mountain Biking

While Fiordland is more known for its rugged terrain, there are areas with trails suitable for mountain biking, including around Te Anau and along certain sections of the tracks.

3.Wildlife Viewing and Photography

Doubtful Sound: This less-crowded fjord offers incredible wildlife viewing opportunities, including dolphins, seals, and even the rare Fiordland crested penguin. You can also take guided boat tours to see these animals up close while enjoying the scenery.

Wildlife

Wildlife in national parks flourishes in a protected environment where ecosystems remain undisturbed by urban development and human interference.


Explore Wildlife

Wildlife animals are species of animals that live and thrive in their natural habitats, usually without direct influence or domestication by humans. These animals can be found across various ecosystems, including forests, oceans, grasslands, deserts, and wetlands. Some examples of wildlife animals include:
1.Lions -
Majestic predators native to African savannas and grasslands. The largest land mammals, found in Africa and parts of Asia.
2.Bengal Tigers -
A powerful and endangered species found in the jungles of India.
3.Giraffes -
Known for their long necks, these herbivores roam the African savannah.
4.Penguins -
Flightless birds that live in the Southern Hemisphere, especially in Antarctica.
5.Monkeys -
These intelligent creatures can be found in forests and jungles across the world, like the Amazon or Southeast Asia.
6.Kangaroos -
Iconic marsupials found in Australia’s outback and forests.
7.Polar Bears -
Found in Arctic regions, they are adapted to cold environments.
8.Red Foxes -
Common in temperate regions, foxes are adaptable and are often found in forests, grasslands, and even urban areas.
Wildlife plays a crucial role in maintaining the balance of ecosystems. However, many wildlife species are currently threatened by human activities, such as deforestation, climate change, and poaching, which has led to a rise in conservation efforts worldwide.

Scenic Drives

Scenic drives offer a captivating way to explore the beauty of the natural world, allowing travelers to enjoy stunning landscapes from the comfort of their vehicle.


Scenic Drives

Scenic drives are some of the best ways to experience nature's beauty and enjoy long, peaceful journeys through picturesque landscapes. Here are a few famous scenic drives across the world:

1.The Garden Route (South Africa)

Location:
Southern coast of South Africa
Highlights:
This coastal route offers spectacular views of cliffs, beaches, and forests. Notable stops include the town of Knysna, Tsitsikamma National Park, and the Outeniqua Mountains.

2.The Atlas Mountains (Morocco)

Location: Morocco
Highlights:
This drive takes you through Morocco’s rugged Atlas Mountains, offering views of high passes, traditional Berber villages, and fertile valleys. It’s a perfect way to explore the country’s diverse landscapes.

3.Trollstigen (Norway)

Location:
Norway
Highlights:
One of Norway’s most famous drives, Trollstigen is a mountain pass with sharp turns, steep inclines, and breathtaking views of waterfalls, valleys, and fjords.

Camping

Camping in a national park offers an immersive experience that allows you to connect with nature in its purest form. Whether you're pitching a tent under towering trees.


Camping Activities

Camping in Fiordland National Park is an incredible experience, offering some of the most stunning and remote wilderness in New Zealand. It's a place known for its dramatic fjords, rainforests, lakes, and towering mountain peaks. Whether you’re an avid adventurer or someone looking for a peaceful escape in nature, Fiordland has something to offer. Camping Options in Fiordland National Park

Department of Conservation (DOC) Campsites

New Zealand's Department of Conservation manages a range of well-maintained campsites in Fiordland. These campsites provide basic facilities (toilets, water, and picnic tables) and are perfect for those who want to experience the wild beauty of Fiordland without a lot of frills. Some popular DOC campsites in Fiordland include: Cascade Creek Campsite (near Lake Te Anau) Mirror Lakes Campsite (close to the famous Mirror Lakes viewpoint)

Freedom Camping

Fiordland National Park has some areas where freedom camping is allowed (camping without designated sites). However, it’s important to check local regulations, as certain areas are restricted, and freedom camping is allowed only in certain designated spots. Be mindful of the “Leave No Trace” principles to keep the environment pristine.

Fishing

Fishing is a peaceful and rewarding activity that allows people to connect with nature.it's casting a line in tranquil lake,river, or the vast ocean, fishing offers a sense of relaxation.


Fishing

Fishing in Fiordland National Park is a fantastic way to experience the region’s natural beauty while enjoying some excellent freshwater fishing opportunities. Fiordland is home to many pristine rivers, lakes, and fjords, offering a variety of fishing experiences for both beginners and seasoned anglers. Here’s everything you need to know about fishing in Fiordland:

Types of Fishing in Fiordland:

Lake Fishing (Freshwater)

Fiordland has several stunning lakes where anglers can fish for species like brown trout and rainbow trout. Some of the most popular lakes for fishing in the park include
Lake Te Anau:
The largest lake in the South Island, known for its brown and rainbow trout. You can fish from the shore, a boat, or try fly fishing.
Lake Manapouri:
Famous for its large brown trout, this lake offers both shore-based and boat fishing opportunities.
Lake Pukaki:
A smaller, scenic lake that holds both brown and rainbow trout. Lake Wakatipu: Known for its beautiful views and abundant fish populations.
